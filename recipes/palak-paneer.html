Palak Paneer Recipe

Description

As winter arrives, there's a culinary tradition that unites families in a shared love for comforting meals, one of which is the timeless Palak Paneer Recipe. A dish that transcends generations, this winter-perfect recipe beautifully captures the essence of the season, particularly when fresh saag (spinach) graces our kitchens. The vibrant green fresh spinach, delicately paired with the velvety creamy goodness of paneer and the aromatic blend of MAGGI Masala-ae-Magic, creates a symphony of flavours that everyone, from the little ones to the elders, looks forward to. THE HISTORY OF PALAK PANEER RECIPE IN INDIA Palak Paneer ki sabji, an iconic Punjabi dish, is also known as Paneer Chenna in some regions. Originating from the traditional Saag Paneer enjoyed in Punjab's cooler months, this dish evolved into Palak Paneer to suit diverse palates beyond the region. Let's get cooking with the Palak Paneer Recipe.

Ingredients

  • 500gms Spinach (chopped)
  • 2 cups Water
  • 2 tbsp Oil
  • 1 teaspoon Cumin Seeds
  • 1 teaspoon Ginger-Garlic Paste
  • 2 Onions
  • 2 Tomato (Medium, Chopped)
  • 2 Green Chillies (Finely Chopped)
  • 1 teaspoon Dry red chillies (Byadagi)
  • 250 gms Paneer
  • 1 Pinch Salt (To Taste)
  • 1 Inch Ginger (Grated)

Steps

  1. Boil the spinach in 1 cup of water till the leaves are wilted. Drain the water and grind the spinach leaves into a fine paste. Keep it aside.
  2. Heat oil in a karahi, add cumin seeds and when they splutter add ginger and garlic and saute for a minute.
  3. Add chopped onions and fry them till golden brown. Now add the tomatoes and saute till soft.
  4. Add green chillies, turmeric powder and red chilli powder. Fry for a minute till the masalas are well mixed.
  5. Add pureed spinach and the remaining 1 cup of water.
  6. Mix well, cover and cook for a minute on low flame.
  7. Add the paneer cubes and cook till the mixture comes to a boil.
  8. Adjust salt to taste. Turn off the flame and garnish with ginger juliennes. Serve hot with rotis.
