Shahi Paneer

Description

Shahi means royal in Hindi. Soft paneer chunks cooked in a thick and creamy gravy ensure this recipe is nothing short of true royalty! But this royal heritage also leads some to believe that it's impossible to cook authentic Shahi Paneer at home. However, making Shahi Paneer in the comfort of your own kitchen is easier than you might think, as long as you have the right ingredients and follow the recipe correctly. So, don't wait...it's time to give it a try!

Ingredients

  • 1 Onion (chopped)
  • 3 Tomatoes (chopped)
  • 1/2 Ginger (ground)
  • 6 Cloves Garlic (crushed)
  • 2 tbsp Butter
  • 1 spoon Coriander Power
  • 1/2 spoon chilli power
  • 1/2 spoon Fenugreek seeds
  • 200 gm Paneer (cut into half-inch pieces)
  • 1 cup water
  • 3 tbsp cream
  • 1 spoon Ginger (ground)
  • 3 tbsp Coriander Leaves
  • 2 Green chillies (slit)
  • 1 Pinch Salt

Steps

  1. Prepare the ingredients as mentioned in the list.
  2. Pressure cook the onion, tomato, ginger and garlic together with 1/2 cup water. Cool and grind into a smooth paste.
  3. Heat butter in a pan. Add coriander powder, red chilli powder, fenugreek leaves and paneer pieces. Saute for a few minutes.
  4. Add the onion-tomato paste, 1/2 cup water and salt to the paneer. Bring to a boil and simmer for 5 minutes. Then add 2 tbsp of cream and let it simmer for 2 minutes.
